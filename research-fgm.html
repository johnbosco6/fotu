Female Genital Mutilation (FGM)

Understanding the epidemiological impact and improving care.

Project Overview

Female Genital Mutilation (FGM) affects millions of women worldwide, yet the long-term obstetrical and psychological consequences remain under-documented in many healthcare systems. My research aims to map these outcomes through large-scale epidemiological data.

View Related Publications

Key Findings

  • Identified specific obstetrical complications correlated with Type III FGM.
  • Developed new survey methodologies for culturally sensitive data collection.
  • Influenced regional health policy guidelines regarding maternal care for survivors.
